Let's Visit a Yosakoi Festival!

There are many yosakoi festivals, both in Japan and more recently internationally!

Are you itching to pull together a team and join in the fun? Check out the rules of participation at the Kochi Yosakoi Festival!



Japan

Naturally Japan is host to the most frequent, most reliable, and biggest yosakoi festivals. For anyone interested in yosakoi or dance alone these festivals are a treat for the senses, often featuring hundreds of dancers in a rainbow of costumes and any number of music styles, most or all of which featuring naruko(clackers) held in both hands. The original and best known festival is the Kochi Yosakoi Matsuri(festival), though the Sapporo Yosakoi-Soran festival may certainly be a match in terms of sheer size. Multiple smaller festivals take place in towns and cities across the country. If planning a visit to Japan in the summer, one of these festivals should definitely be on your must-see list!

  • Kochi Yosakoi Matsuri

    • Held in Kochi, on the island of Shikoku in southwestern Japan in August each year.
    Visit the Kochi City visiters' guide to learn more!

  • Sapporo Yosakoi-Soran Matsuri

    • Held in Sapporo, on the northern island of Hokkaido in June of each year. This festival features a fushion of yosakoi and native soran dance styles.
    Visit the official Yosakoi-Soran Festival homepage to learn more!
    Note: It seems that COVID-19 may have derailed the festival, but due to its size and popularity I imagine it will be back soon!

  • Nippon Domannaka Matsuri

    • Held in Nagoya, effectively the "dead center"("domannaka") of Japan. This festival is commonly known by its nickname, "Domatsuri". This festival is held at the end of August each year.
    Visit the official Domatsuri homepage to learn more!

  • Harajuku Omotesando Genki Matsuri Super Yosakoi

    • Held in Harajuku in the heart of Tokyo, this festival is also held at the end of August each year. This is likely the most accessible festival for most travelers.
    Visit the official Super Yosakoi homepage to learn more!

  • Koiya Matsuri

    • This festival is held in Osaka, Japan's second largest city, on the western shore of the main island, in the shadow of the world-famous Osaka Castle. Unlike many of the other festivals, this one takes place in mid-September.
    Visit the official Koiya homepage to learn more! (This page is only in Japanese, but automatic translation is available in Chrome and other web browsers).



US

While there are no festivals dedicated to yosakoi (yet!), yosakoi may be seen at several cherry blossom festivals.

  • Northern Cherry Blossom Festival (San Francisco/Bay Area) - April
  • Sakura Matsuri Cherry Blossom Festival (New York City) - April



Malaysia

Yosakoi performances are held in March of each year in Penang by the Pink Hibiscus Yosakoi Dancers as a way to strengthen the ties between Japan and Malaysia.



Vietnam

The Japan Sakura Matsuri is held each year in Hanoi and usually features yosakoi performances, with ten teams from Vietnam and Japan participating. Other Japanese-centric festivals, such as anime conventions, may also feature yosakoi.

Brazil

Sao Paolo is well known as the city with the highest density of Japanese culture outside of Japan owing to an influx of workers in the late 1800s. The city has occasionally held yosakoi festivals, though it is unclear (to the author) whether they are ongoing. In 2005 the festival was held in July.




Groups and universities around the world, including the US, Canada, and across Europe may also have access to yosakoi teams, but do not have any specific festivals that they participate in.

Useful Terms to Know

Within the world of yosakoi, there's a lot of words you'll probably come across; these are the most common. I've done my best to give a approximate pronunciation for each word based on English sounds for those with little experience with the Japanese language, but remembering that there are only five vowel sounds (ah, ee, oo, eh, oh) will help you with any other word you may come across!


yosakoi (yo-sah-coy)
"come at night"; a dance originating in Kochi Prefecture
naruko (naw-roo-ko)
wooden handheld clackers, often painted red
bosan (boh-sun)
a monk
kanzashi (kawn-zah-she)
a woman's hair comb
tabi (taw-bee)
split-toe shoes
matsuri (mott-soo-ree)
festivals
hachimaki (hah-chee-mah-kee)
headbands
